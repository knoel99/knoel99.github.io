Why This Paper Exists

Test-time scaling — letting models "think longer" at inference — has been the dominant recipe for reasoning gains. But this recipe has a hidden tax: every extra token of reasoning multiplies the cost of attention, which scales quadratically. The authors identify this as the binding constraint: you could scale test-time compute further, but the attention mechanism itself becomes the bottleneck before you ever reach the model's cognitive ceiling.

DeepSeek-V4's central thesis is that compression is the path to scale. Rather than making attention faster through hardware alone, they make it smaller — aggressively compressing the KV cache through two complementary strategies. Think of CSA as a smart summary (compress, then attend sparsely to what matters) and HCA as a rough sketch (compress heavily, but attend densely to the sketch). Together, they let a 1M-token context window become computationally tractable — not by working harder, but by working on compressed representations.

The two-model strategy (Pro at 1.6T/49B, Flash at 284B/13B) reflects a deliberate trade-off: Pro chases frontier quality, Flash chases frontier efficiency. Both share the same architectural DNA, meaning the compression innovations pay dividends regardless of scale. The post-training pipeline — cultivating domain experts independently, then merging via on-policy distillation rather than simple averaging — is designed to avoid the "jack of all trades" dilution that plagues unified fine-tuning.

Why "compress then attend" rather than "attend then compress"?

The ordering is critical. If you attend first (standard attention), you've already paid the O(n²) cost before any compression can help. By compressing the KV cache before attention, the quadratic operation runs on a much shorter sequence. CSA compresses along the sequence dimension and then applies sparse attention on top — a double reduction. HCA takes the opposite trade-off: compress more aggressively (fewer tokens retained) but keep dense attention on what remains, preserving full connectivity at the cost of more information loss. The hybrid design lets the model allocate its "attention budget" across both strategies, using each where it's most effective.

The FP4 precision on routed experts is a forward-looking bet: current hardware treats FP4×FP8 the same as FP8×FP8 in peak throughput, but future hardware could theoretically deliver a 1.3× efficiency gain — meaning V4's efficiency numbers will improve on tomorrow's chips without any architecture change.
