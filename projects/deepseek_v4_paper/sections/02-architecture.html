Architecture — The Stability Playbook

This section is the engineering backbone of V4: it defines what changed from V3, what stayed, and why the single most important structural addition — mHC — exists to solve a problem that vanilla Hyper-Connections created at depth. The broader context is that V4 is not a radical redesign; it's a targeted upgrade where the authors identified exactly which components bottlenecked scaling and replaced only those.

Think of standard residual connections as a highway with one lane. Hyper-Connections (HC) widen that highway to nhc lanes, letting the model learn richer skip-path mixing. But without traffic rules, signals amplify or cancel unpredictably across layers — the highway becomes unstable. mHC is the traffic law: by constraining the residual mixing matrix Bl to the Birkhoff polytope (doubly stochastic matrices), the authors guarantee that no signal can grow unboundedly — every row and column of Bl sums to 1, so the transformation is inherently non-expansive. The Sinkhorn-Knopp algorithm is the differentiable projection that enforces this constraint.

The MoE changes are deliberately conservative — switching the affinity function from Sigmoid to Sqrt(Softplus(·)), removing routing-target constraints, and adding Hash-routed MoE in early layers — because the MoE architecture was already well-tuned in V3. The authors' priority was clear: don't destabilize what works; fix what breaks at scale.

Why doubly stochastic matrices guarantee stability

A doubly stochastic matrix has every row and column sum to 1 with all non-negative entries. This means:

  • Spectral norm ≤ 1: the largest singular value is bounded by 1, so applying Bl never amplifies a vector's magnitude. Across L layers, the product BL…B1 also has spectral norm ≤ 1 because the Birkhoff polytope is closed under multiplication.
  • No signal cancellation: combined with Sigmoid-constrained Al and Cl (non-negative, bounded), the residual path can only mix or attenuate — never flip signs or explode.
  • Sinkhorn-Knopp is the standard differentiable projection onto this manifold: alternate row and column normalization on exp(B̃l) until convergence. 20 iterations suffice in practice.
